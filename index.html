Let's set up your personal Macbook password!

At this point, you should be logged in to VPN, OKTA & Jamf.
They're pre-requisites for below steps.
  • Click on the jamf Icon on top right corner
  • On the drop down list, select the change password option
  • Enter the old and new password to complete change
  • Done

    • Create a new password

    First things first, lets set up your Windows password!

    • On the Windows desktop screen press CTRL + ALT + DEL.
    • Click on Change a password.
    • Create your new password following the rules below.
    Password rules:
  • At least: 10 characters long, 1 uppercase , 1 lowercase, 1 number.
  • Can’t include any variation of your name.

    • The old password is the initial password that you used to login the first time.

    Creat a new Bitlocker Password

    • Click on Windows Start button in the botton left corner of the screen.
    • Click on This PC icon.
    • When in Windows explorer, right-click in Local Disk (C:) and click Change Bitlocker Password.
    • Create your new pin, use the same one as your newly made Windows password and click on Change PIN.
    Password rules:
  • At least: 10 characters long, 1 uppercase , 1 lowercase, 1 number.
  • Can’t include any variation of your name.

    • The Bitlocker password should be the same password you set up for your Windows account.

    Connect to VPN

    • First of all, did you notice the QR code in the email you received with your account and initial password? So this is your Wise VPN token, scan this QR code with Google Authenticator.
    • Open 'Cisco AnyConnect' that you can find in the dock at the bottom of your screen.
    • Fill the blank field with the address vpn.transferwise.com click Connect.
    • Fill your Username and Password, these are the same ones that you used to login to your computer, and then click on Ok.
    • Fill the 'Answer' field with the token from Google Authenticator app in your phone.

    Connect to VPN

    • First of all, did you notice the QR code in the email you received with your account and initial password? So this is your Wise VPN token, scan this QR code with Google Authenticator.
    • Open 'Cisco AnyConnect' that should be running in Windows and can be found in the in the bottom right of the screen, in the icon bar in the task bar.
    • Fill the blank field with the address vpn.transferwise.com and click 'Connect'.
    • Fill your Username and Password, these are the same ones that you used to login to your computer, and then click on 'Ok'.
    • Fill the 'Answer' field with the token from Google Authenticator app in your phone.
    You will have two different token numbers in your Google Authenticator app, one for your Wise Google account and another for VPN, make sure you are using the correct one!

    Logging in to Slack

    • Click here to launch your Okta apps
    • Click on the Wise slack app and Join the Wise workspace
    • This should launch slack in your browser
    • Now open the Slack desktop app on your taskbar(Windows) or dock(Mac).
    • Click on the "Sign In to Slack" button.
    • A Chrome tab will open again, now click on the "open in slack"
    • This should redirect you to the slack desktop app.

    You can ask your teammates or team lead to add you to required work channels.
    Your favorite shortcut will be CTRL + K(Windows) or Command + K(MAC).
    This will pop up a "Jump To" Window, where you can easily search for someone or channel.

    Log in to your Wise Email

    • Open Google Chrome and go to 'www.gmail.com'. Once on the gmail login page please type 'name.lastname@transferwise.com' and click 'Next'. This will lead you to Okta log in page.
    • Once on the Okta log in page use 'firstname.lastname' in the username section. Use the same password that you used to login to your laptop.
    • Complete the Okta login process and you should be redirected to your gmail inbox
    Okta is responsible for the connection to your Google account and many other services in Wise. To see which services you have in your Okta account click -> here

    If you want, you can have more then one 2fa set up for you Okta account, like Google Authenticator and Okta Verify, but at least one is mandatory the keep your access safe!

    Headset set up

    • Plug the microphone into the female microphone jack on the ear cup and push until the microphone sits flush
    • Connect the detachable headset cable, by plugging the headset part into the female jack on the left headset ear cup
    • Then plug the other end of the detachable headset cable to your laptop.
    • Done

    Make sure to check the control box to adjust the volume and unmute the microphone during use.

    Let's sync up chrome with your Wise email

    • Click on the head and shoulder icon on top right corner.
    • Click on "Turn on sync".
    • Enter your Wise email.
    • Then click Next and enter your Okta credentials.
      username firstname.lastname@transferwise.com
      password: same as your laptop password
    • Click Sign in. This should take you to the Okta page.
    • On the okta page, it may request you to enter the okta auth code from google Authenticator
    • Click continue on the "Verify that it's you" page.
    • Then, Link data -> yes, I'm in.

    Jamf login and okta 2fa set up

    • Click on the Jamf icon on the top right corner of your screen

      • Note: At this point, you should be connected to VPN as per previous point 'Connect to VPN'. If not, please check point 1.

    • Next, on the jamf connect dialogue box, enter your credentials and click ‘Sign In'

      • username firstname.lastname
      password: same as your VPN/laptop password

    • Enter same credentials (username & password) and click ‘Sign In'.
    • Scroll down to google authenticator and click 'set up'.
    • Choose your phone OS type, scan QR code and enter an Okta token number from your google authenticator.
    • Cick verify, scroll down and click 'finish'.

    At this point, you should have two different token numbers in your Google Authenticator app, one for VPN and another for OKTA, make sure you are using the correct one!

    Mouse Set up

    First of all, M720 mouse don't need a dongle to connect. They connect via bluetooth

    • Open bluetooth settings from icon on the task bar
    • Select the "add a device" option
    • On the "add a device" page, click on the plus sign and select the first option (bluetooth)
    • Select the "add a device" option

    • Next, press and hold down a number on the mouse till it starts to blink very fast
    • The mouse name "M720 Triathlon" should then appear on the device list (on your screen)
    • Select it and click on Done.

    Logging into Zoom

    • Open the zoom app from your dock/task bar
    • Click on the SSO option on bottom left
    • Then enter "transferwise" in the company domain box
    • Click Continue and done.
