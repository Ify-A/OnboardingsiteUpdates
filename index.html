Let's set up your personal Macbook password!

Connect to VPN



Sign-in to Jamf Connect

  1. Click on the Jamf icon on the top right corner of your screen

  2. Next type in your Okta credentials and click ‘Sign In

  3. Enter your 2fa code for Okta which should be found in Google Authenticator and click ‘OK’

  4. After logging in, you will be redirected to your Okta account in your browser.

Resetting with 2fa

  1. Click -> here to visit the 2fa page.

  2. Type in your username (Firstname.Lastname) and your OTP and click ‘Submit’

  3. Type in your new password and click ‘Update

    4. Remember
  4. Passwords need to be at least 10 characters long and include, 1 uppercase, 1 lowercase, 1 number and cannot include any variation of your name.


Finally, sync

  1. Sign back into Jamf Connect as before
  2. Once signed back into Jamf Connect and if everything has gone correctly, you will be prompted to sync your passwords. Type in the Local Password and click ‘Sync’


    3. Local password means:
  3. The password that you use to login to your laptop. If today is your first day at Wise then this will most likely be the password sent in your onboarding email.

New computer password

First things first, lets set up your Windows password!

  1. On the Windows desktop screen press CTRL + ALT + DEL.
  2. Click on Change a password.
  3. Create your new password following the rules below.
Password rules:
  • At least: 10 characters long, 1 uppercase , 1 lowercase, 1 number.
  • Can’t include any variation of your name.

    • The old password is the initial password that you used to login the first time.

    New Bitlocker Password

    Setting up a new Bitlocker password:

    1. Click on Windows Start button in the botton left corner of the screen.
    2. Click on This PC icon.
    3. When in Windows explorer, right-click in Local Disk (C:) and click Change Bitlocker Password.
    4. Create your new pin, use the same one as your newly made Windows password and click on Change PIN.
    Password rules:
  • At least: 10 characters long, 1 uppercase , 1 lowercase, 1 number.
  • Can’t include any variation of your name.

    • The Bitlocker password should be the same password you set up for your Windows account.

    Connect to VPN

    Connecting to VPN:

    1. First of all, did you notice the QR code in the email you received with your account and initial password? So this is your Wise VPN token, scan this QR code with Google Authenticator.
    2. Open 'Cisco AnyConnect' that you can find in the dock at the bottom of your screen.
    3. Fill the blank field with the address vpn.transferwise.com and click 'Connect'.
    4. Fill your Username and Password, these are the same ones that you used to login to your computer, and then click on 'Ok'.
    5. Fill the 'Answer' field with the token from Google Authenticator app in your phone.
    You will have two different token numbers in your Google Authenticator app, one for your Wise Google account and another for VPN, make sure you are using the correct one!

    Logging in to Slack

    1. First, launch the chrome browser, then click on the head and shoulder icon on top right corner.
      • Click on "Turn on sync".

      • Enter your Wise email.
      • Then click Next and enter your Okta credentials.
        username firstname.lastname@transferwise.com,
        password: same as your laptop password
      • Click Sign in. This should take you to the Okta page.


    2. On the okta page, it may request you to enter the okta auth code from google Authenticator
      (In most cases, it shouldn't)
      • Click continue on the "Verify that it's you" page.
      • Then, Link data -> yes, I'm in.


    3. To confirm your chrome is synced
      • Click on your name initial at top right corner of the address bar, and check if sync is on


    4. Once chrome syn is complete, click on the Slack icon on your taskbar(Windows) or dock(Mac).
      • When Slack opens, click on the "Sign In" button.


    5. A Chrome tab will open, click on "Continue with Google" and choose your TrasnferWise email account on the next page.
    6. Next, please choose the Wise Workplace option, and click on. "Open".


      • A box should pop-up at the top of the page, click "Open slack


    7. Now your Slack app should open automatically logged in to your account.
      • Please click on the picture icon on the top right corner and select "Edit profile" on the list.
      • Fix your full name, add as much information as you can and don’t forget to upload a picture of yourself so other Wisers can know who you are!
      • When done, click on 'Save changes'.

    Ask your teammates or team lead to add you to required work channels.

    In slack, your favorite shortcut will be CTRL + K(Windows) or Command + K(MAC). This will pop up a "Jump To" Window, where you can easily search for someone or channel.

    Log in to your Wise Email

    Logging into email (Okta 2fa set up)

    1. Logging into your Wise email is done through Okta but first you'll need to set up your 2 factor authentication.
    2. Open Google Chrome and go to 'www.gmail.com'. Once on the gmail login page please type 'name.lastname@transferwise.com' and click 'Next'. This will lead you to Okta log in page.
    3. Once on the Okta log in page use 'name.lastname' in the username section. Use the same password that you used to login to your laptop.
    4. As this is the first time you are logging onto Okta, you will have to create 2fa (2 factored authentication) for your account. The simpler method is Google Authenticator, but you can also use Okta Verify.
    5. If you choose Google Authenticator, choose the device (Android or Iphone), scan the QR code in the Google Authenticator app. Next, Insert the code from the Authenticator app. Click on 'Verify' and you should have your 2fa set up!
    6. Click on finish and you will be automatically redirected to your gmail inbox.
    Okta is responsible for the connection to your Google account and many other services in Wise. To see which services you have in your Okta account you can access https://transferwise.okta-emea.com/app/UserHome.

    If you want you can have more then one 2fa set up for you Okta account, like Google Authenticator and Okta Verify, but at least one is mandatory the keep your access safe!
